Paneer Tikka Masala

Paneer is an Indian cheese that is made from the combination of milk and an acidic vegetable such as the juice of a lemon. This is my favorite dish and can never get tired of it! This dish is best served with either roti/naan or rice.

To learn more about how to make paneer from home, please visit my YouTube channel. For this recipe, I am using short cuts for us busy individuals – so I am using a store-bought paneer.

Paneer Preparation

We are going to marinate our paneer slices in yogurt and a bunch of spices:

  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • ½ tsp garlic paste
  • ½ tsp grated ginger
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/8 teaspoon amchur powder (optional)

Mix it all up and let this sit for about an hour – the longer the better!

Now to prepare the curry

  1. In a pan on medium flame, add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil. When the oil is hot, add ½ tbsp cumin seeds.
  2. Once the seeds are sizzling, add 1 large onion, chopped along with 3 chopped tomatoes. Add ½ tsp garlic paste.
  3. Let them cook for about 2 minutes and then begin to add the following spices: ½ tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp garam masala, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ¼ tsp amchur powder (optional)
  4. Stir until they are well combined until and let it cook for about 10 minutes. Occasionally sauté them to prevent them from sticking to the pan or burning.
  5. Once the raw smell of the spices have been cooked off, turn off the heat and let it cool on the side. Once the curry has cooled down, transfer them to a blender and blend it up until smooth!

Tip: You can add soaked cashews (I soaked my overnight) in this mixture as well – this is what brings the creaminess of the curry.

Now that our paneer has been marinated, it’s time to lightly pan fry them. Add ½ tablespoon of oil on a pan and fry the paneer pieces. Flip them to get all sides fried.

Back to our main dish, transfer the blended curry mixture into the pan with the paneer pieces and add salt to taste and let it cook for a few more minutes before taking it off the stove! Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with naan/roti or basmati rice.
